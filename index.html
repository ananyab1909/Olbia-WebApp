Olbia is a city in northeast Sardinia, Italy. It's known for the medieval San Simplicio basilica and for cafes dotting central squares like Piazza Matteotti. On the palm-lined waterfront to the east, the Musea Archeologico di Olbia has exhibited ranging form Nuragic artifacts to Roman warships.
